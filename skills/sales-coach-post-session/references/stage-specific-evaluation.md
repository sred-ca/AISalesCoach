# Stage-Specific Evaluation Criteria

This document defines what "good" looks like at each stage of the SRED.ca sales pipeline. The weekly-prep skill uses these criteria to evaluate Evan's performance and flag deals that need attention.

## Pipeline Stages (HubSpot IDs)

| Stage | HubSpot ID | Description |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Opportunity | 30153821 | Initial contact — qualified lead, first meeting scheduled or completed |
| SR&ED Assessment | appointmentscheduled | Assessment call completed, determining if SR&ED fit exists |
| Technical Discovery | 31821993 | Deep dive into client's R&D activities with technical team |
| Follow-Up | contractsent | Proposal or engagement letter sent, awaiting decision |
| Closed Won | closedwon | Client signed, engagement begins |
| Closed Lost | closedlost | Deal did not proceed |

## Opportunity Stage

**What should happen here:**
- First meeting (intro/discovery call) with the prospect
- Evan determines if the company does qualifying R&D work
- Basic fit assessment: industry, team size, R&D spend indicators

**Evaluation criteria:**
- Is Evan leading the conversation or deferring to Jude/Logan?
- Did Evan ask discovery questions about the prospect's R&D activities?
- Was a clear next step established (SR&ED Assessment call)?
- Follow-up email sent within 4 hours?

**Stale threshold:** 14 days without activity
**Red flag:** No follow-up email after first meeting

## SR&ED Assessment Stage

**What should happen here:**
- Deeper assessment call to determine SR&ED eligibility
- Review of the prospect's projects for technological uncertainty and advancement
- Payment gate: Stripe Letter of Engagement (LOE) must be sent and confirmed

**Evaluation criteria:**
- Did Evan prepare for the assessment by reviewing the company's work?
- Quality of questions: surface-level ("Do you do R&ED?") vs. deep ("Walk me through a project where you hit a technical wall")
- Was the Stripe LOE sent? Was it confirmed/signed?

**Payment gate rule:** Do NOT schedule Technical Discovery until Stripe LOE is received and confirmed. Flag any deal at this stage where LOE status is unknown.

**Stale threshold:** 21 days without activity
**Red flag:** Moving to Technical Discovery without confirmed LOE

## Technical Discovery Stage

**What should happen here:**
- Deep technical dive, usually with Logan (technical lead) present
- Document specific R&D projects, experiments, uncertainties
- Evan's role: lead the conversation, frame the SR&ED narrative, keep it on track

**Evaluation criteria:**
- Is Evan leading or is Logan running the call?
- Evan's talk ratio should be 30-50% (not silent, not dominating)
- Are follow-up questions specific to the client's domain?
- Is Evan capturing enough technical detail for the SR&ED claim?

**Stale threshold:** 30 days without activity
**Red flag:** Evan silent during technical discovery (talk ratio <15%)

## Follow-Up Stage

**What should happen here:**
- Proposal or engagement letter sent to the prospect
- Active follow-up to address questions and close
- This is where deals stall most often — Evan needs persistence without being pushy

**Evaluation criteria:**
- How many follow-up touches this week? (email, call, LinkedIn)
- Is Evan personalizing follow-ups or using templates?
- Is there a clear next step, or just "checking in"?
- Does Evan create urgency? (filing deadlines, fiscal year timing, competitor awareness)

**Stale threshold:** 14 days without activity
**Red flag:** Generic "just checking in" emails with no value-add

## Closed Won

**What to note:**
- Celebrate the win in the coaching report
- Document what Evan did well in the sales cycle
- Note the total cycle time (Opportunity → Closed Won)

## Closed Lost

**What to note:**
- Why did it close lost? (prospect's decision, timing, fit, Evan's performance)
- Was there a coaching opportunity? (could better discovery or follow-up have changed the outcome?)
- Don't dwell — note it factually, move on

## Cross-Stage Patterns to Watch

**Velocity:** How fast are deals moving through stages? Slowing velocity = problem.

**Stage clustering:** If most deals are at one stage (e.g., 5 at Follow-Up), Evan may be moving deals forward but not closing. Coach on closing skills.

**Drop-off points:** Where do deals go to die? If most Closed Lost come from Follow-Up, the issue is persistence or urgency creation. If from Assessment, the issue is qualification.

**Payment gate compliance:** Track LOE status religiously. Deals that skip the payment gate create downstream problems (work done without commitment).
